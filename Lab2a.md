## Reflection

Based on the TCO analysis done above, the laser printer would be a wiser choice in a 5 year run. Based on the load that is required of the printer, it does not make sense to use an inkjet as it would be running at maximum capacity most times causing it to spoil faster than the laser printer that was made for handling heavier loads. For a home user, their requirements for a high performance printer is not necessary and hence the inkjet would be a more recommended solution for them. If cost is a concern, the home user can also open for ink cartridges that are catered for small loads (like 100 pages) rather than the larger cartridges. Non-financial factors that influence what printer to buy would probably look at space given for the printer. Larger printers tend to take up more space in a room whereas a small printer can be on a table top / sitted on a cupboard as long as there is sufficient space and a proper electricity source. Another concern to look at is the convenience of connecting to a printer, while some printers require user to be connected to a desktop / have wired connection to a printer, ease of use like bluetooth connectivity/wi-fi connectivity also plays a big role in considering if it is a suitable fit for a particular person/family/organisation. Thirdly, the amount of sound that the printer makes when printing a document. In a household, there is a lower tolerance for loud noises when someone is performing a print as compared to organisations which may have a dedicated print room and any sound from that room would impact the other users minimally. 

Given such a load, the Brother printer would catch up to the Canon printer in about 12 weeks to break even. 

In a organisation scale, it would be a wiser decision to get laser printers. First and foremost, speed of printing is very important especially for meetings that have bigger groups and materials need to be printed as fast as possible. Secondly, with a laser printer, there are more functions. Users are able to connect to the printers on any level as long as they are within the organisations network. This can also an important factor when the company would like to restrict certain users to focus black and white printing on one printer and coloured prints on another device instead to ease the workflow of their employees
